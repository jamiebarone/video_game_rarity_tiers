Video Game Rarity Tiers * Based on total copies sold *

A simple rarity scale with examples to assist with accurate game classification.
Remember, when you see a game described as 'Rare': 'Rare' is rarely rare!

HOLY GRAIL

Under 1,000 copies
  • Original production was extremely limited (e.g., competition prizes)
  • Distribution was disrupted or copies were destroyed, recalled, or never widely sold
  • Atari Air Raid Blue Cart (12-20 copies)
  • NES Nintendo World Championships 1990 Gold Cart (26 copies)
  • NES Nintendo World Championships 1990 Gray Cart (90-116 copies)
  • NES Stadium Events (200-300 copies)
  • PS2 Final Fantasy X - Square Enix Variant (500-800 copies)

RARE

1,001-10,000 copies
  • Limited or interrupted production, often due to publisher collapse or poor early sales
  • Commercial release occurred, but in very small numbers
  • NES Cheetahmen II (1,500-2,000 copies)
  • NES Bonk's Adventure (~10,000 copies)
  • PS5 Concord (~10,000 copies sold before production shut down)

SCARCE

10,001-50,000 copies
  • Commercially released
  • But sold poorly, released late, or appealed to niche audiences
  • Genesis Musha (10k-15k copies)
  • NES Little Samson (10k-20k copies)
  • GBC Shantae (25k-40k copies)
  • NES Mega Man 5 (~50k copies)

UNCOMMON

50,001-100,000 copies
  • Mass-produced
  • But didn't sell well or had limited distribution windows
  • SNES Mega Man 7 (70k-100k copies)
  • SNES Final Fight Guy (80k-100k copies)
  • SNES Chrono Trigger (~100k copies)

COMMON

100,001-1,000,000 copies
  • Mass-market releases
  • Wide retail distribution, often for multiple years
  • SNES Castlevania IV (500k-700k copies)
  • SNES Mega Man X (~1m copies)

EXTREMELY COMMON

1,000,000+ copies
  • Games that defined their systems
  • Only 'rare' when you isolate specific variants
  • NES Super Mario Bros. (40m copies)
  • SNES Super Mario World (20.6m copies)
  • Wii Wii Sports (82m copies)
  • PS2 Final Fantasy X (8.5M world-wide)
  • GC Legend Of Zelda: Wind Waker (4.6M)
  • GC Legend Of Zelda: Ocarina of Time (7.6M)

PAPER RARE (aka 'Artificial Grail')

Generally under 2,000 copies
  • Purposfully manufactured with in low numbers to appeal to collectors.
  • Rare on paper, not due to gaming's organic chaos (such as recalls, weird distribution, destroyed stock, etc.).
  • Anything manufactured by Limited Run Games, Super Rare Games, Premium Edition Games, etc.
  • Homebrew/hobbyists produced games
  • PS4 Poop Slinger (84 copies)
  • SNES Shadowhawk (36 copies)
Note 1: Rarity classification can change depending on region, variant, condition, CIB/sealed, etc.
Example 1: PS1 Final Fantasy X sold 5.3m units in North America, categorizing it as EXTREMELY COMMON. But the black-label "Square Enix" variant only sold between 500-800 copies, which puts it in the HOLY GRAIL category.
Note 2: Placement on this list does not dictate fair market value.
Example 1: N64 Ocarina of Time sold 7.6 million copies (EXTREMELY COMMON). Opened, CIB, gray cartridge variants sell between $300-$500 depending on condition; opened, CIB, gold cart variants between $400-$1400. On the other hand, sealed copies of PS5 Concord, which only sold 10K (upper end RARE, lower end SCARCE), sell for $20-60.
Example 2: With some exceptions, generally any Greatest Hits/Players Choice/Platinum Edition variants sell less than first issue variants.
